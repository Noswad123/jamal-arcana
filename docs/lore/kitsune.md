# Kitsune

A Kitsune is a many-tailed companion of shifting rooms, remembered layouts, and clever returns.

Where a Waystone remembers a place, a Kitsune remembers how a working den was arranged: which rooms were open, which panes were listening, which commands were waiting, and how the Operator moved between them.

## Classification

- Native
- Creature

## Veiled Form

- A multiplexer companion for named, composable working-session kits. The daily command is `kit`, with current Herdr support for doctor checks, capture, restore, smart navigation, validation, and an early TUI. The local app workspace is `~/Projects/kitsune`.

## Arcane Form

- A fox-spirit familiar that learns the shape of an Operator's workspace and can return that shape on command.
- Each kit is a remembered den: a stack of workspaces, tabs, panes, and commands bound into a reusable form.
- Its cleverness is not raw power, but arrangement. A Kitsune can slip between pane, tab, and workspace boundaries because it understands the hidden paths between them.

## Relationships

- Works beside [Tmux](./tmux.md) and Herdr-like multiplexers as a companion rather than a replacement.
- Complements [Waystone](./waystone.md): Waystone anchors where work lives; Kitsune remembers how work was arranged.
- Complements [Wisp](./wisp.md): Wisp briefly floats a single invocation; Kitsune restores an entire den of ongoing work.
- May borrow disciplined movement patterns from [Aerospace](./aerospace.md), especially when panes and windows must return to precise positions.

## Lore Notes

- A Kitsune may grow a new tail whenever it masters another backend, such as Herdr, tmux, or Zellij.
- Capture is memory; restore is shapeshifting.
- Smart navigation can be framed as the fox choosing whether to move through the pane-world or pass the key onward to the creature inside it.
