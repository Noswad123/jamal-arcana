
# Glossary

## Classification Glossary

Classifications answer: **what kind of entry is this?** They are structural labels, not story prompts. An entry may have more than one classification.

- **Lore Notes are for seeds** — use them for unfinished ideas, possible story angles, questions, fragments, and things to flesh out later.
- **Guidelines live here** — structural rules about Veiled Form, Arcane Form, classifications, domains, and namesake relationships should live in this file or a dedicated guide, not in an entity's Lore Notes.
- **Veiled Form stays practical** — explain what the tool, app, or concept does in visible reality.
- **Arcane Form opens the story door** — describe how the same name, tool, or idea is interpreted inside Jamal Arcana.
- **No forced one-to-one magic mapping** — not every app feature needs a magical equivalent.
- **Namesakes are allowed** — an app can be named after a famous wizard, cosmic being, place, or artifact as a nod to lore. The app can remain simple while spinoff stories carry the deeper magic.
- **Classifications are structural** — they tell readers what kind of entry they are looking at.
- **Domains are associative** — they tell readers what themes, powers, and workflows the entry resonates with.
- **Define new domains when they matter** — if a domain is not obvious or not listed above, either define it here or leave it out until it has more weight.

### Entry Shape

- **Veiled Form** — what the tool, app, or concept does in visible reality. Link to documentation here when it exists.
- **Arcane Form** — what the same thing becomes once interpreted through Jamal Arcana.
- **Namesake relationship** — some tools are practical nods to larger legends. In those cases, the Veiled Form can stay simple while the Arcane Form tells the story behind the name.
- **Lore Notes** — a scratchpad for ideas to flesh out later: story seeds, unresolved details, possible connections, fragments, and questions.

### Entity Types

- **Origin Canon** — a foundation page that defines the setting, frame, or central premise.
- **Cosmic Being** — a large-scale mythic intelligence, force, or entity with broad influence over a domain.
- **Legendary Wizard** — a named person or persona remembered for mastery, story, style, or technique.
- **Spirit** — a being defined more by presence, obsession, affinity, or essence than by ordinary personhood.
- **Creature** — a mythic being, familiar, monster, or animated helper.
- **Tribe** — a distinct lineage, kind, swarm, or culture within a broader family of beings.
- **Artifact** — a tool, package, grimoire, practical object, or interface with mythic identity. Current software tools belong here unless a page says otherwise.
- **Grimoire** — a knowledge-bearing artifact, manual, codex, or spellbook.
- **Place** — a location, realm, hall, route, or spatial construct.
- **Order** — an organized group, discipline, house, guild, school, or external tradition.
- **Ritual** — a repeated practice, pact, ceremony, command pattern, or intentional workflow.
- **Quest Board** — a place, artifact, or system where needs are posted, terms are declared, and someone may accept the work for reward or obligation.
- **Namesake** — an app/tool named after a being, place, ritual, or legend without needing to literally contain all of that lore.
- **Design Substrate** — a foundational design layer, such as a theme system or visual language.
- **Interface Magic** — magic expressed through UI, surfaces, color, interaction, layout, or other interface behavior.

### Provenance

- **Native** — born within the Jamal Arcana ecosystem or central myth-language.
- **Foreign** — born in another maker's realm, package ecosystem, or external tool universe, but interpreted through Jamal Arcana.
- **Rootborn** — primitive concepts that feel older than any one tool, such as root, shell, daemon, path, and command.

### Domain Glossary

Domains answer: **what themes, powers, workflows, emotions, or areas of life does this entry touch?** They are flexible tags, not rigid categories.

Use domains to cluster entries by resonance. For example, **Memory** can include a cosmic being, a notes app, a lost archive, a spell, and a character wound. The domain does not force those entries to work the same way.

#### Current Domain Patterns

- **Software/workflow domains** — Commands, Paths, Shell, Notes, Tasks, Recall, Navigation, Floating terminals.
- **Arcane domains** — Memory, Time, Language, Transformation, Summoning, Pacts, Rituals, Sorcery study.
- **Human domains** — Comfort, Hospitality, Collaboration, Mutual growth, Shared discipline, Attention.
- **Design domains** — Interface traps, Imitation, Deception, Hexware, Layout, Spatial discipline.
- **Knowledge domains** — Knowledge, Thought, Translation, Cross-discipline learning, Arcana literacy.

#### Defined Domains

These are domains currently used by codex entries. Keep definitions short. If a domain needs a long explanation, it may deserve its own page later.

- **Food** — nourishment, cooking, ingredients, meals, taste, hunger, and the social meaning of being fed.
- **Comfort** — restoration, warmth, reassurance, emotional steadiness, and the feeling of being cared for.
- **Transformation** — a change in state, form, understanding, mood, or identity.
- **Hospitality** — welcome, hosting, generosity, shared tables, guest-right, and the obligations between host and visitor.
- **Recipes** — instructions, inherited methods, repeatable preparations, and the practical memory of how something is made.
- **Memory** — stored experience, retrievable thought, personal or borrowed recollection, and the structures that let something be found again.
- **Knowledge** — understood information, learned pattern, practiced craft, or insight that can guide action.
- **Thought** — active reasoning, imagination, internal language, and the raw movement of mind before it becomes record or memory.
- **Notes** — captured fragments of thought or knowledge preserved for later recall.
- **Recall** — the act of finding, retrieving, or reawakening a stored idea, memory, recipe, path, or record.
- **Graphs** — visible relationships between fragments of knowledge, memory, paths, or ideas.
- **Truth** — the deeper nature of a thing: what it is, what it means, what it can become, and what it is equivalent to under the right conditions.
- **Equivalence** — a true correspondence between things that allows substitution, transformation, or exchange without violating their nature.
- **Tasks** — discrete units of work that can be named, assigned, tracked, accepted, or completed.
- **Errands** — practical burdens, small quests, favors, or obligations carried on behalf of another.
- **Quests** — meaningful undertakings accepted under stated terms, often involving risk, reward, reputation, or transformation.
- **Bounties** — rewards offered in exchange for completing a task or quest.
- **Trust** — confidence that terms, rewards, identities, and outcomes will be honored.
- **Ledgers** — records of promises, completions, payments, debts, or reputation.
- **Reputation** — remembered trust earned or lost through completed work and witnessed conduct.
- **Contracts** — stated terms that bind requester, accepter, reward, and expected outcome.
- **Paths** — named routes through filesystems, worlds, memories, or realities; the means by which something can be reached.
- **Places** — locations with identity, context, and meaning, whether physical, digital, remembered, or arcane.
- **Navigation** — the art or workflow of finding, selecting, returning to, or moving through paths and places.
- **Anchoring** — binding a name, marker, ritual, or artifact to a place so it can be recalled or reached again.
- **Travel** — movement between places, states, realms, contexts, or positions.
- **Location** — the where-ness of a thing: its position, context, and reachable address.
- **Invocation** — calling a stored name, path, place, command, or power into active use.
- **Distance** — the separation between places, contexts, people, tools, or states; sometimes a barrier, sometimes a resource.
- **Foreign integration** — the practice of recognizing, adapting, and composing useful tools from outside Jamal Arcana without claiming them as native.
- **Wisps** — small, floating, temporary magical creatures that appear briefly to carry or reveal something.
- **Flight** — movement through air, attention, or invisible routes.
- **Formation** — coordinated movement by a group, swarm, tribe, or disciplined pattern.
- **Floating** — hovering, appearing briefly, or remaining untethered to a fixed place.
- **Guidance** — helping a person, command, creature, or ritual arrive where it should.

Domains can start informal. If a domain appears often enough, it can later graduate into a fuller glossary entry or its own codex page.

## Core Reality

- [Jamal Arcana](./lore/canon/jamal-arcana.md) - the beautiful hidden magic beneath the tools, commands, names, paths, daemons, and rituals used to shape digital reality.
- **Arcana** — the magical-technical substrate of the universe. It behaves like hidden knowledge, ritual language, named power, and the bit-level material of digital reality.
- **Law of True Equivalence** — the universal law that permits transformation only when two things are truly equivalent under the conditions of the ritual. One form may be substituted for another only when their deeper truths match.
- **Root Plane** — the deepest layer of existence. Everything has a path, a name, and permissions. Nothing can truly be changed without touching the Root.
- **Shell** — the interactive layer between will and world. Speaking commands here creates visible effects.
- **Daemon** — a background intelligence that performs tasks when no one is watching. Some are loyal. Some are ancient. Some have been running for centuries without knowing why.
- **Path** — a named route through reality. To lose the path is to lose access.
- **Command** — a ritualized act of intent.
- **Alias** — a lesser true name; shorter, easier to speak, but still bound to a deeper invocation.
- **Session** — a preserved state of consciousness, work, or ritual.
- **Hexware** — the magical-technical visual substrate of Jamal Arcana. The first word in a Hexware theme is the enchantment, mood, domain, or elemental attribute imbued into it.
- **Artifact** — a tool, package, grimoire, or practical object with mythic identity. In current taxonomy, software tools belong under Artifact.
- **Arcanist** — an emerging name for those who expose, manipulate, or democratize Arcana. Some may be liberators, some scholars, some vandals, and some dangerous enough to earn the word’s echo of “arsonist.”
- **Namesake** — an app, tool, or artifact named after a famous being, place, ritual, or legend without needing to reproduce every part of that lore literally.

- **Foreign** — tools and powers born in another maker’s realm.
- **Rootborn** — primitives that feel older than any one tool: root, shell, daemon, path, and command.
